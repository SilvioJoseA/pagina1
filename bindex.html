HISTORY

Get to know our history.

„My name is Byamukama Levi, I initiated Byamukama Levi Foundation in 2013 as a non-profit, child-focused, Community Based Organization whose task is to care for children living in desperate poverty in Kabale district, Southwestern Uganda. Many children in this district are going through a lot of problems that are beyond their control. This area is inhabited by persons providing sexual services who give birth to children, but they do not educate them and do not meet other basic needs necessary for the child's development, as a result of which these children end up on the streets of Kabale town, begging for survival chance. Others come from families whose parents died of AIDS and were unable to find a foster family that could provide them with basic needs. Others come from broken families, others have parents, but live in deep poverty. Because of this, many children do not go to school at all, and some of them dropped out of school prematurely due to lack of money for fees and other school needs, such as books, uniforms, food, etc. That is why the Byamukama Levi Foundation was founded, to reduce some of the problems that these children face.”

WHAT WE DO

Our mission

Our main goal is “to produce sustainable improvement in the lives of vulnerable children, their households, and to serve as resource or model in communities.” We support the poorest children and provide them with access to free and good quality education, including school accessories and uniforms. We strive to provide conditions conducive to effective teaching of children. We have a strategy for ensuring a long-term sustainability of our programs, through donor diversification, mobilization of government founding, community investment, income-generating, activities and other creative measures.

WHAT'S NEXT?

Our plans for the future

The projects main objectives will be achieved through the following activities:

  • Building of a children’s centre that will consist of a school, hostel and a clinic (to provide health services to the children and the entire community).
  • Promotion of health, education and peace through provision of scholastic and sanitary materials support, and engagement of children in games and sports leagues activities.
  • Improvement of food and nutrition capacity in the beneficiary households through training on good productive activities to vulnerable family households.
  • Enhancing knowledge to caregivers and the community members on the basic needs and right of a child, and policies that protect children.
  • Identifying and training of Child Rights Promoters who will be functional and promoting child protection in their communities.

WHO WE HELP?

„The potential possibilities of any child are the most intriguing and stimulating in all creation.” Ray L. Willbur 

Our help is directed mainly to children whose life and material situation do not allow them to function normally in society and prevent access to education. We strive to ensure that children in Kabala have equal opportunities and through our activities we enable them to start a better start in the adult life. Kabale has the worst development index in Uganda in multiple categories: education, literacy, nutrition, access to health and income. Many children in Kabale district have growth disorder, some of them severely.

Therefore, The Byamukama Levi Foundation has introduced The School Demonstration Farm Project to address vulnerability in Kabale. Its aim is to:

  • raise funds to sustain the school using the profits from selling the farm products,
  • reduce food supply instability and to improve the nutrition of local population to combat famine and malnutrition, especially among the youngest,
  • train caregivers in best practices to improve on the household income and nutrition.

As Kabale shifts away from dependency on handouts into a productive mode, the vulnerable households will continue to require protection and specific targeted interventions to guarantee their income, food and nutrition security, and access to basic services.

This is where The School Demonstration Farm Project comes in. The School Demonstration Farm Project will enable vulnerable individuals and households to afford and access basic services, but also give them the platform from which they can start activities that will eventually lead the out of poverty.

Regular income enables people to meet their basic needs for food, medical care and access to education. This means that the hopelessness of living “one day at a time is addressed” and people can start thinking in longer term, for example by investing in assets, that will increase their income in time.

Provided that vulnerable households are trained on productive activities, they will not be subjected to severe shock and thus the transformation from one economic to another will be softened.

The School Demonstration Farm Project is the answer to this issue by offering a perfect transition to economic independence for the Kabale people. The School Demonstration Farm Project contributes to the core of human development. With the regular income, the everyday nutrition, education and medical care standards can surely improve.

The benefits of increasing the local population income will result in:

  • Improving the nutrition of the local population - because people will be able to buy better and more nutritious food. They will be empowered to plan for what to eat.
  • Increase in school attendance – people will no longer have to pull their children out of school because no school appliances (books, pens, pencils, uniform) are available to them. This means improved school completion rates, and a better educated population.
  • Increased interest in medical attention. As a result the local population becomes healthier and therefore the economic productivity of the population is also elevated.

This project helps alleviate poverty and addresses vulnerability, it promotes productivity and empowers populations. In longer terms, it breaks the transmission of poverty from one generation to the next, therefore contributing to long term social and economic transformation.
